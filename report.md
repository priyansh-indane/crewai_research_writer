In 2024, quantum computing reached new heights with Google’s breakthrough in quantum advantage, solving a chemistry simulation in minutes—a task that would take classical supercomputers millennia. Recent advances in quantum error correction have created logical qubits with extended coherence, pushing fault-tolerant quantum computing closer to reality. Hybrid quantum-classical processors now blend quantum chips with traditional silicon, enhancing computational power. Additionally, topological quantum computing is gaining traction through experiments manipulating Majorana fermions, promising more stable qubits. Meanwhile, quantum machine learning is emerging as a transformative field, with new algorithms tackling complex pattern recognition and optimization problems beyond classical capabilities.
